K-Nearest Neighbors



1. Data

$$ \mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,1} & x_{1,2} & \cdots & x_{1,d} \\ x_{2,1} & x_{2,2} & \cdots & x_{2,d} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n,1} & x_{n,2} & \cdots & x_{n,d} \end{bmatrix} \quad \mathbf{y} = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \\ \vdots & \\ y_n \end{bmatrix} $$

Notice : n is the number of data points and d is the number of dimensions



2. Input - Model - Training - Prediction

Input : $$data \:\: \mathbf{X}(n \times d) \:\: , \:\: label \:\: \mathbf{y}(n \times 1) \:\: and \:\: k \:\: (nearest \:\: points) $$
Model: $$ Saving \:\: data \:\: \mathbf{X} \:\:, \:\: label\:\: \mathbf{y} \:\: and \:\: k$$
Training: $$ Saved \:\: data \:\: \mathbf{X} \:\:, \:\: label\:\: \mathbf{y} \:\: and \:\: k$$

Prediction for a new data point (\(\mathbf{z}\)) :
Caculating distance from a new data (\(\mathbf{z}\)) to each data (\(\mathbf{x_i}\)) $$ \mathbf{z} = \begin{bmatrix} z_{1} & z_{2} & \cdots & z_{d} \end{bmatrix} \qquad \mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,1} & x_{1,2} & \cdots & x_{1,d} \\ x_{2,1} & x_{2,2} & \cdots & x_{2,d} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n,1} & x_{n,2} & \cdots & x_{n,d} \end{bmatrix} \Rightarrow \mathbf{distance} = \begin{bmatrix} \mathbf{z}-\mathbf{x}_1 \\ \mathbf{z}-\mathbf{x}_2 \\ \vdots \\ \mathbf{z}-\mathbf{x}_n \end{bmatrix} $$ Chosing label of (\(\mathbf{z}\)) based on (k) nearest (\(\mathbf{x_i}\))



3. Caculating the distance (L2)

$$ \mathbf{D} = \| \mathbf{z}- \mathbf{x_i} \|_2^2 $$ $$ \qquad \qquad \:\: = (\mathbf{z}- \mathbf{x_i})(\mathbf{z}- \mathbf{x_i})^T $$ $$ \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \:\:\: = \mathbf{z} \mathbf{z}^T - \mathbf{z} \mathbf{x_i}^T - \mathbf{x_i}\mathbf{z}^T + \mathbf{x_i}\mathbf{x_i}^T $$ $$ \qquad \qquad \qquad \: = \| \mathbf{z} \|_2^2 + \| \mathbf{x_i} \|_2^2 - 2\mathbf{z}\mathbf{x_i}^T $$ Notice : We can ignore \(\|\mathbf{z}\|_2^2\) and calculate \(\|\mathbf{x_i}\|_2^2\) before to save time of computing

Linear Regression



1. Data

$$ \mathbf{X} = \begin{bmatrix} x_{1,1} & x_{1,2} & \cdots & x_{1,d} \\ x_{2,1} & x_{2,2} & \cdots & x_{2,d} \\ \vdots & \vdots & \ddots & \vdots \\ x_{n,1} & x_{n,2} & \cdots & x_{n,d} \end{bmatrix} \quad \mathbf{Y} = \begin{bmatrix} y_1 \\ y_2 \\ \vdots & \\ y_n \end{bmatrix} $$

Notice : n is the number of data points and d is the number of dimensions


2. Model

Function for a data point :

$$ f(x) = \hat y \approx y $$ $$ \, $$ $$ \hat y = x_1w_1 + x_2w_2 + x_3w_3\: + \:...\: + \:x_dw_d + w_0 $$


3. Mathematics

Vector x for a data point : $$ \mathbf{x} = [1,\: x_1,\: x_2,\: x_3,\: ...,\: x_d] $$

Vector w : $$ \mathbf{w} = [w_0,\: w_1,\: w_2,\: w_3,\: ...,\: w_d]^T $$

So : $$ \hat y = \mathbf{x}\mathbf{w} $$

Loss Function for a data point : $$ L(\mathbf{w}) = \hat y - y $$ $$\qquad \quad \:\:\:\: = (\hat y - y)^2 $$ $$\qquad \qquad \:\:\:\: = \dfrac{1}{2} (\hat y - y)^2 $$ $$\qquad \qquad \qquad = \dfrac{1}{2} (\mathbf{x}\mathbf{w} - y)^2 $$

Loss Function for all data points : $$ L(\mathbf{w}) = \dfrac{1}{2} \sum_{i=0}^{N} (\mathbf{x_i}\mathbf{w} - y_i)^2 $$ $$\quad = \dfrac{1}{2} \| \mathbf{X}\mathbf{w} - \mathbf{y} \|_2^2 $$ So : $$ \mathbf{w} = \arg\min_{\mathbf{w}} L(\mathbf{w}) $$


Solve by the gradient method :
Using this gradient formula : $$ (\: \| \mathbf{A}\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{b} \|_2^2 \:)' = 2\mathbf{A}^T(\mathbf{A} \mathbf{x} - \mathbf{b}) $$ So : $$ \dfrac{\partial L(\mathbf{w})}{\partial \mathbf{w}} = \mathbf{X}^T(\mathbf{X} \mathbf{w} - \mathbf{y}) $$ Solve by the zezo gradient method : $$ \mathbf{X}^T(\mathbf{X} \mathbf{w} - \mathbf{y}) = 0 $$ $$ \qquad \qquad \: \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X} \mathbf{w} = \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{y} $$ $$ \qquad \qquad \: (\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X})^{-1} \: \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X} \mathbf{w} = (\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X})^{-1} \: \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{y} $$ $$ \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \:\:\:\: \mathbf{w} = (\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X})^{-1} \: \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{y}$$


4. Data - Model - Prediction

Data : $$data \quad \mathbf{X}(N \times d) \quad and \quad target \quad \mathbf{y}(N \times 1) $$ Model : $$ (\mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{X})^{-1} \: \mathbf{X}^T \mathbf{y} $$ out put is $$ \mathbf{w}(d \times 1) $$ Prediction : $$ \hat y = x_1w_1 + x_2w_2 + x_3w_3\: + \:...\: + \:x_dw_d + w_0 $$
